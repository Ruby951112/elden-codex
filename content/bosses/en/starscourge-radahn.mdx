## Phase Strategy

Radahn is spectacle first, boss second. The developers designed this as a *festival* — you are meant to summon the eight warriors scattered across the arena and let them share the burden. Fighting Radahn solo is possible but misses the point entirely. This is a war of attrition where you are the commander, not the frontline.

The arena is vast and Radahn opens with ranged attacks. Your first objective is not to fight him — it is to summon allies.

### Phase I — The Conqueror of the Stars

As you enter, Radahn fires gravity arrows from horseback. These arrows arc high and track aggressively. Sprint perpendicular to him in wide circles — never straight toward. The arrows explode on impact with heavy knockback.

> **Critical:** Eight golden summon signs are scattered across the dunes. Summon all of them before engaging Radahn directly. The NPCs include Alexander, Blaidd, Okina, and five others. They can be re-summoned if they die — their signs reappear after 30 seconds.

Once your army is assembled, Radahn will aggro onto them. Stay at mid-range and punish his recovery frames. His horseback attacks are wide but telegraphed:

- **Gravity arrow barrage** — Four arrows in rapid succession. Roll toward him on the third arrow to close distance.
- **Sword drag sweep** — He drags both swords along the ground while charging. The hitbox is deceptively wide. Roll perpendicular, not backward.
- **Gravity slam** — Purple energy gathers around his swords. He slams the ground, creating a shockwave. Jump over the wave or roll through it at the last moment.

Radahn's horse, Leonard, is slow to turn. Circle-strafing clockwise forces him to reposition constantly, giving your summons free hits from behind.

### Phase II — The Meteor Strike

At ~50% HP, Radahn plants his swords in the ground and launches himself into orbit. The screen fades — this is your window. **Heal fully, re-summon any dead NPCs, and apply buffs.** The meteor phase gives you 8-10 seconds of prep time.

When he returns, he crashes down as a meteor with massive AOE damage. Sprint perpendicular to his trajectory the moment you see the meteor icon appear on screen. The impact zone is 15 meters wide — do not gamble on dodging through it.

Phase 2 introduces new attacks:

- **Gravity pull** — He raises one hand and drags you toward him while charging a thrust. Spam roll backward to resist the pull.
- **Rock barrage** — Four gravity boulders orbit him before launching outward. Stay close to him; the rocks miss at melee range.
- **Meteor dive (second use)** — If the fight drags on, he can launch into orbit again. Same counter: sprint perpendicular.

The NPC summons will die faster in phase 2. Re-summon them immediately when their signs reappear. Every second Radahn targets an NPC is a second you survive.

## Attacks & Counters

| Attack | Counter |
|--------|---------|
| Gravity arrow (opening) | Sprint perpendicular in wide arcs. Do not roll — the tracking adjusts. |
| Sword drag sweep | Roll perpendicular to his charge. The hitbox extends 3 meters to either side. |
| Gravity slam | Jump over the shockwave or roll forward through it. Punish with 2 R1s. |
| Meteor dive (phase transition) | Sprint perpendicular the moment you see the falling meteor icon. Never stop sprinting. |
| Gravity pull + thrust (P2) | Roll backward repeatedly to resist the pull. Roll left on the thrust. |
| Rock barrage (P2) | Stay within 5 meters of him. The rocks curve outward and miss at close range. |
| Dual sword combo (P2) | Four-hit string ending with overhead slam. Roll into him on hits 2 and 4. |

## Recommended Loadout

Radahn is one of the few fights where your build matters less than your strategy. The NPC summons deal significant damage — you are there to assist, not solo carry.

- **Spirit Ash:** None needed. The eight NPC summons replace spirit ashes entirely.
- **Weapon:** Anything with reach. **Great Epee**, **Bloodhound's Fang**, or **Moonveil** excel. Radahn is immune to bleed but vulnerable to **Scarlet Rot** — Antspur Rapier with rot grease trivializes the fight.
- **Talismans:**
  - **Dragoncrest Greatshield Talisman** (physical damage reduction)
  - **Great-Jar's Arsenal** (equip load — his attacks stagger light builds)
  - **Green Turtle Talisman** (stamina regen for sprinting)
  - **Crimson Amber Medallion +1** (HP buffer)
- **Items:** **Preserving Boluses** for Scarlet Rot (the arena is covered in rot pools). **Rainbow Stones** to mark safe zones if you lose track of positioning.
- **Bow/Crossbow:** Optional but helpful. Radahn can be chipped from range while NPCs tank. Serpent Bow with poison arrows adds passive damage.

> **Rot Strategy:** Apply Scarlet Rot once via Rotten Breath incantation or rot grease. Let the rot tick while you play defensively. Re-apply when the status wears off. Two full rot procs remove ~40% of his health.

## Torrent Usage

Torrent is available during this fight — the only remembrance boss where this is true. Use him for three purposes:

1. Sprinting across the dunes to summon NPCs at the start.
2. Dodging the meteor dive in phase 2 (Torrent's sprint is faster than your roll).
3. Repositioning when Radahn aggros onto you mid-combo.

Do NOT fight from horseback. Torrent's hitbox is larger and Radahn's sweeps will clip you constantly. Summon him, reposition, dismount, fight on foot.

## Lore Pause

Radahn is the last living demigod who still holds his sanity — barely. He learned gravity magic to spare Leonard, his scrawny horse, from his own weight. The festival is not a celebration; it is a mercy killing. Every warrior here respects him enough to grant him an honorable death.

When he launches into the stars during phase 2, he is not retreating — he is showing you the same technique he used to *halt the stars themselves* at the height of his power. The meteor dive is his legacy, weaponized.

> *Radahn is not a villain. He is a tragedy.*

## Post-Fight Unlocks

Defeating Radahn unlocks:

- **Remembrance of the Starscourge** — Trade for **Starscourge Greatsword** (paired colossal swords with gravity AOE) or **Lion Greatbow** (fires gravity arrows).
- **Nokron, Eternal City** access — A crater opens in Mistwood, granting entry to the underground. This is required for Ranni's questline.
- **Radahn's Great Rune** — Raises maximum HP, FP, and stamina by 15% when activated at Dragonbarrow Divine Tower.

## Related Bosses

Radahn is the third demigod most players face (after Godrick and Rennala). If you struggled here, the difficulty curve resets — Leyndell's bosses are more technical but less punishing than this DPS check. If you beat Radahn, you are ready for the capital.
