## Phase Strategy

Alecto is not just difficult — she is a precision test. Unlike the standard Black Knife Assassins scattered throughout the Lands Between, Alecto fights with the aggression and intelligence of a player invader. She chains combos endlessly, closes distance instantly, and punishes panic rolls with deadly accuracy. This is a solo duel in the truest sense: evergaols forbid spirit summons. You must win clean.

The arena is small and featureless. There are no pillars to hide behind, no cliffs to exploit. Alecto will hunt you down within seconds of any attempt to create space. The fight demands constant engagement — the moment you disengage to heal, she closes with her lunge or leaping slash.

> **Critical:** Alecto's attacks deal percent-based HP damage, meaning high Vigor provides minimal protection. A single mistimed roll often leads to death regardless of your stats. This fight is won through pattern recognition, not build optimization.

### Understanding Her Rhythm

Alecto operates in burst windows. She commits to 2-4 hit combos, then pauses for 0.5-1 second before her next string. Your punish windows exist only in these pauses — never mid-combo. The key insight: **roll toward her, not away**. Her slashes track forward aggressively but have poor hitbox coverage behind her leading shoulder.

Her most dangerous trait is her **frame advantage** — she recovers from attacks faster than you recover from rolls. If you roll too early, she will catch you in recovery. If you roll too late, the attack connects. The timing window is 4-6 frames (0.13-0.2 seconds at 30fps). This is tighter than any mandatory boss in the base game.

Most players attempt Alecto around level 60-80 and fail spectacularly. The recommended approach: return at 90-100 with a +18-20 weapon after clearing most of Liurnia and Caelid. The rune reward (32,000) and her ashes (Black Knife Tiche, the best summon in the game) are worth the wait.

## Attacks & Counters

| Attack | Counter |
|--------|---------|
| Triple slash combo | Roll forward-right on slash 2. Do not roll on slash 1 — it is a feint. Punish with 1 R1 after slash 3. |
| Death blade lunge | She drags her blade low, then thrusts. Roll toward her left side (your right) the instant the blade glows red. |
| Leaping overhead slash | She jumps backward, then forward. Roll forward into her as she descends. The landing has no follow-up. |
| Blade of Death (ranged projectile) | She crouches and fires a wave. Strafe-walk perpendicular. Do not roll — she can fire a second wave mid-roll. |
| Grab (red eyes, both hands extended) | Triggered only if you are within 3 meters for more than 2 seconds. Sprint backward immediately. Cannot be rolled. |
| Backstep cancel into re-engage | She feints retreat, then lunges. Do not chase. Wait for her to commit, then roll into her second lunge. |

### The Death Blade Lunge — Extended Notes

This is her deadliest attack. Alecto drags the blade horizontally across the ground while charging forward. The visual cue is subtle: a faint red glow at her hip, 0.3 seconds before she moves. The hitbox extends 2 meters to her right (your left), making left-side rolls a coin flip.

The safest counter: roll forward-right at the moment the glow intensifies. You will pass through her left shoulder's dead zone. If you roll too early, she tracks. If you roll backward, the lunge reaches 6 meters and will catch you.

After landing the lunge, Alecto recovers in 0.8 seconds. You have time for exactly one light attack. Do not greed a second — she will punish with the grab.

### The Grab — Instant Death Mechanic

If Alecto's eyes glow red and she extends both hands, sprint away immediately. The grab has a 3-meter range, ignores iframes, and deals 90% of your max HP on connection. There is no follow-up — if she misses, you have a 2-second window for healing or aggression.

The grab is only used if you remain close to her for extended periods without attacking. The game interprets passive circling as turtling and triggers the punish. Stay mobile. Attack intermittently to reset her AI priority.

## Recommended Loadout

Because spirit ashes are forbidden, your loadout must compensate for the lack of aggro splitting. The meta strategy revolves around bleed:

- **Weapon:** **Uchigatana +18** with **Seppuku** Ash of War, or **Rivers of Blood +9**. Alecto has low poise and bleeds in 4-5 hits. A single bleed proc removes 15% of her HP — three procs effectively shorten the fight by half.
- **Talismans:**
  - **Dragoncrest Greatshield Talisman** — reduces physical damage by 20%. Her percent-based attacks still hurt, but this buys you one extra mistake.
  - **Green Turtle Talisman** — stamina regeneration. You will be rolling constantly.
  - **Crimson Amber Medallion +1** — slight HP buffer.
  - **Claw Talisman** (optional) — boosts jump attack damage. If you struggle with dodge timing, jump-heavy attacks have more generous iframes.
- **Items:**
  - **Boiled Crab** (consumable, +20% physical resistance) — purchased from Blackguard at Liurnia Boilprawn Shack. Stacks with Dragoncrest.
  - **Ironjar Aromatic** (optional) — short-duration hyper-armor. Use during her backstep feints for guaranteed punishes.

For sorcery builds, **Rock Sling** is the only viable strategy. The projectiles stagger her reliably, and the cast time is fast enough to avoid her lunge punishes. Pair with **Terra Magica** for damage boost.

Faith builds struggle here. Most incantations are too slow. **Dragonmaw** works if you can bait her grab and punish the whiff, but the risk is extreme.

## Why This Fight Matters

Defeating Alecto unlocks **Black Knife Tiche**, the single most powerful spirit ash for late-game and NG+ content. Tiche is faster than the Mimic Tear, deals percent-based HP damage, and dodges attacks with near-player intelligence. She trivializes Malenia, Maliketh, and Mohg.

The fight also serves as a skill gate. If you can beat Alecto at level 90, you are ready for the endgame. If you cannot, the Mountaintops of the Giants and beyond will destroy you. Consider this an audition.

> *Alecto does not forgive hesitation. She does not allow spacing. She is the closest Elden Ring comes to a Sekiro duel.* Treat her like one — parry with rolls, counter with precision, and never greed.

## Alternate Strategy — Patience Build

If bleed fails and frustration mounts, pivot to the defensive setup:

- **Fingerprint Stone Shield +20** with **Shield Crash** Ash of War
- **Great-Jar's Arsenal** talisman (carry heavier gear without fat-rolling)
- **Barricade Shield** Ash of War on a secondary shield for stamina stability

With this build, block her first two slashes in every combo, then counter-attack on the third. She will still chip through the shield, but damage is reduced by 90%. The fight becomes a war of attrition — boring, but effective for players who refuse to learn dodge timings.

Most players report this method takes 8-12 minutes per attempt versus 2-4 minutes with bleed. Your tolerance for tedium will dictate the choice.

## Related Bosses

After Alecto, the Black Knife Assassin variants in Ordina Liturgical Town and the Consecrated Snowfield will feel trivial. The dodge timings you master here translate directly to **Maliketh, the Black Blade** in Crumbling Farum Azula — he shares the same death-blade lunge animation and timing windows.
