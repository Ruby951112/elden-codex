## Phase Strategy

The Tibia Mariner is less a boss fight and more a mounted chase scene. Unlike traditional arena encounters, this skeletal boatman drifts across the Liurnia coastline on a spectral vessel, summoning skeleton reinforcements while retreating from direct confrontation. The fight is trivial if you understand one rule: **never dismount**.

### Phase I — The Skeletal Fleet

The Mariner spawns on the eastern Liurnia shoreline, patrolling a shallow water stretch between the cliffs and the lake. When you approach, it immediately summons 2-3 skeleton soldiers and begins sailing away from you in a wide arc. This is the entire fight.

> **Critical:** Mount Torrent before engaging. The Mariner's movement speed exactly matches yours on foot — you will never catch it without your horse. The entire encounter is designed around mounted hit-and-run tactics.

The skeleton adds are the only real threat. They spawn in waves of 2-4 and will chase you aggressively. There are two valid approaches:

1. **Ignore them entirely** — Sprint past on Torrent, strike the Mariner, then circle out of skeleton range. The adds despawn when the Mariner dies.
2. **Kill them quickly** — Torrent's charge attack (hold sprint + R1) will one-shot most skeletons. This is safer but slower.

The Mariner itself has no direct attacks. It casts a purple fog occasionally that summons more skeletons, but it will not swing at you, breathe fire, or guard itself. Your only enemy is your own patience.

## Attacks & Counters

| Attack | Counter |
|--------|---------|
| Skeleton summon (3x wave) | Sprint past them on Torrent. Do not stop to fight. |
| Purple fog ritual | The Mariner stops to cast. Free 2-3 hits while it channels. |
| Retreat sail | It moves in wide circles. Cut across the arc to intercept. |
| Skeletal ambush (shore) | Pre-existing skeletons on the beach join the fight. They do not respawn. |

The purple fog is your punish window. When the Mariner stops to summon, ride directly into it and swing twice. Do not greed — it will resume sailing immediately after the cast finishes.

## Recommended Loadout

This fight does not require optimization. Any weapon works. That said:

- **Mount:** **Torrent** is mandatory. Unlock the Spectral Steed Whistle from Melina at your third Site of Grace.
- **Weapon:** Long reach weapons (spears, halberds, colossal swords) hit from Torrent more reliably. **Flail** and **Winged Scythe** work exceptionally well here.
- **Damage type:** Holy and Fire deal bonus damage, but the fight is so short it rarely matters. A +3 longsword kills it in under a minute.
- **Spirit Ash:** Do not summon. The arena is open water — most ashes drown or fall behind immediately.

If you dismount to fight the skeletons manually, equip **Holy Water Pots** or any AOE incantation. Skeleton packs cluster tightly and die to burst damage.

## Strategy Notes

The Mariner fight teaches you mounted combat fundamentals — specifically, how to strike from Torrent without losing momentum. Practice the following:

- **Charge attacks** (sprint + R1) deal 30% more damage than standing R1s and have longer reach.
- **Jump attacks** from Torrent have massive hitboxes. Time them as you pass the Mariner's boat.
- **Circle strafing** — Ride in a wide loop around the Mariner's path, closing distance only to strike, then pulling away before skeletons converge.

Most deaths in this fight come from players stopping to heal or fight skeletons. The correct strategy is *momentum over damage*. Land one hit per pass, reset, repeat. The Mariner has 1280 HP — you need 8-12 solid strikes maximum.

## Loot and Rewards

Defeating the Tibia Mariner drops:

- **Deathroot** — Required item for Gurranq's questline at the Bestial Sanctum. This is the third Deathroot available in the base game.
- **1800 Runes** — Enough for a single level at early-to-mid game.

The Deathroot is the real reward. Trade it to Gurranq for **Beast Eye**, which alerts you to nearby Deathroot locations, and access to powerful Beast incantations like **Stone of Gurranq** and **Bestial Sling**.

> *If you miss this Deathroot, the next available one is locked behind the Black Knife Catacombs dungeon — a far harder challenge.*

## Related Bosses

The Tibia Mariner appears in multiple regions throughout the game. Each encounter functions identically but with minor environmental differences:

- **Summonwater Village** (Limgrave) — Earliest variant, surrounded by poison pools.
- **Wyndham Ruins** (Altus Plateau) — Spawns on a flat plain with no water.
- **Mountaintops of the Giants** — Final variant, surrounded by revenants instead of skeletons.

All variants drop Deathroot. None are missable — they respawn if you reload the area after resting at a grace.
